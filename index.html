Galiano Island

Road Tips for Visiting Cyclists

Welcome to Galiano! We hope you will enjoy your time here on one of the most beautiful islands in Canada. As a cyclist visiting the island, it is important that you be aware of the unique road conditions on Galiano in order to have a safe visit. Our road space is quite limited, so in order to keep everything functioning normally it would be greatly helpful if you considered the requests below!

Important Tips

  • › When riding in a group, please consider making room to let vehicles pass. Read more.
  • › Please ride single-file with other cyclists, rather than side by side.
  • › Please avoid gathering in driveways.
  • › If riding with a large group, please try to break apart into groups of 3-5 so cars can get around you.
  • › When stopping for a break, please avoid stopping near a corner or crest of a hill so motorists will easily see you.

Our road infrastructure lacks bicycle lanes, which means that cyclists and motor vehicles share the road throughout the island. Additionally, all of our roads accommodate only one lane of traffic heading in each direction. The roads are often winding and hilly with narrow shoulders that make pulling over challenging in some areas.

When motor vehicles find themselves behind groups of cyclists, it is difficult for motorists to pass them. In order to give safe distance to cyclists, motorists need to use the opposite lane when passing. There are limited road segments with enough straight roadway for motorists to do this safely.

Due to these conditions vehicles can become stuck behind groups of bikes for a long time, causing traffic disruption. Unfortunately, there have been attempts to pass unsafely in these situations. With Galiano's growing popularity as a cycling destination, this issue has become more serious.

Please help keep our roads safe

We kindly ask that when cycling groups have a motor vehicle behind them, that they please try to make way. Consider pulling over when it is safe to do so. This is not required by law but your cooperation will contribute greatly to keeping our roads functioning for everyone. Thank you for your consideration!

Make sure to explore all that the island has to offer while you are here. For a detailed layout of rest stops, parks, and landmarks check out this map provided by the CRD! Have a fun visit!
