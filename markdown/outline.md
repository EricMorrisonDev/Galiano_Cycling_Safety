


Galiano Road Safety for Cyclists

Quick Safety Tips:
- When a car is behind you, please pull over and let them pass. Read more...
- Wear visible gear. Avoid dark colors. 
- When stopping for a break, please avoid stopping near a corner or crest of a hill so motorists will easily see you. 


Welcome to Galiano! We hope you will enjoy your time here on one of the most beautiful islands in Canada. As a cyclist visiting the island, it is important that you be aware of the unique road conditions on Galiano in order to have a safe visit. 

Unfortunately our road infrastructure lacks bicycle lanes, which means that cyclists and motor vehicles share the road throughout the island. Additionally, all of our roads accommodate only one lane of traffic heading in each direction. 

When motor vehicles find themselves stuck behind slower moving bicycles, it creates a situation where the motorists cannot safely pass them. Motorists often have no other choice than to pull into the opposite lane while passing cyclists. This creates a dangerous situation where head on collisions could occur. With Galiano's increasing popularity as a cycling destination, this road safety issue has become increasingly serious. 

To address this we kindly ask that when any cyclist has a motor vehicle behind them, that they please pull their bike over and allow the vehicle to pass safely. We understand that this is not required by law, but your cooperation will contribute greatly to keeping our roads safe for everyone.
